REGULATORY FOUNDATION
03 / 16
RBI IRACP · Master Direction · Nov-12-2021

What the regulator actually requires.

The clarification harmonised NBFC asset classification with banks — ending decades of "month-end" reckoning.

Daily stamping
Overdue position must be reckoned at day-end, not month-end. An account is "overdue" if not paid on the due date.
NPA threshold
Asset becomes NPA on 91st day of overdue status, irrespective of part-payment.
Upgrade rule
NPA can be upgraded to standard only on clearance of entire arrears — principal & interest. Single EMI cure does not lift status.
SMA stages
SMA-0: 1-30 DPD · SMA-1: 31-60 · SMA-2: 61-90. Reportable to CRILC for ≥5 Cr.
IndAS ECL
Stage 1 (12-mo ECL) → Stage 2 on SICR or 30+ DPD → Stage 3 on default. Provision multiplier 3-5× from S1 to S2.
Fair Practices
RBI 2022 directions: agent code, recorded calls, no contact 1900-0700, no harassment, grievance escalation in 30 days.
Digital Lending
Cooling-off period, KFS, look-back & reset on regulated lender's books; FLDG 5% cap.
SARFAESI
Available to NBFCs ≥100Cr asset size; secured exposures ≥20L. 13(2)→13(4) timing is the playbook lever.
Implication

Three operating mandates fall out of the rules

  • Real-time DPD engine with day-end stamping, customer-level rather than facility-level cure logic.
  • Cure ≠ partial payment. Collection KPI must be full-arrears resolution, not nominal recovery.
  • Stage migration (SMA-0/1/2) is the leading indicator the board should track — not just GNPA.

What the regulator does not dictate

Channel mix, sequencing, restructure offers, and analytics. This is where competitive advantage compounds.

Section III · Strategic architecture

Six pillars hold up the 0-90 system.

Each pillar has a single accountable owner, a measurable outcome, and a 90-day first deliverable.

i.

Data & Decisioning

Unified customer 360, real-time DPD stamping, propensity-to-default & cure-probability models. Output: a daily action queue, ranked.

ii.

Channel Orchestration

Rules engine choosing channel, intensity, sequence by segment x bucket. Output: cost-to-collect at top-quartile.

iii.

Customer Experience

Tone calibrated to risk, hardship policy codified, RBI Fair Practices hard-wired. Output: NPS in collected pool above −10.

iv.

Workforce & Agency

In-house core for Tier-1; agencies tiered, scored, rotated. Cure-weighted incentives. Output: agency cure uplift 15%+ vs baseline.

v.

Tech Stack

Dialer, WhatsApp BSP, FOS app with geo-tag, dunning engine, dispute desk, repo workflow. Output: single pane of glass for collections.

vi.

Governance

Daily flow huddle, weekly bucket review, monthly NPA-prevention scorecard, quarterly board update. Output: the meeting that runs collections.

Section III · Risk segmentation

Three risk tiers — treated differently, every bucket.

Segmentation built from bureau, behaviour, intent, and macro overlay. Refreshed monthly; cohort-based learning loop.

Tier L · Low risk

"First-slip salaried & secured"

Profile: salaried, clean bureau, mandate healthy historically, single bounce. ~55% of book.

  • Treatment: digital-only first, no FOS
  • Cure target: 80%+ within 1-30 DPD
  • Cost ceiling: <Rs.80/case avg
  • NPS preserved as cross-sell asset
Tier M · Medium risk

"Cyclical self-employed"

Profile: self-employed/MSME, mild bureau stress, repeat 1-30 DPD, seasonal income. ~30% of book.

  • Treatment: digital + tele, FOS at 31+
  • Cure target: 60%+ within 1-60 DPD
  • Restructure offered selectively
  • Co-applicant engaged at 31 DPD
Tier H · High risk

"Stress & intent-flagged"

Profile: thin file, bounce history, geography/industry stress, fraud flags, broken PTPs. ~15% of book, drives 70% of losses.

  • Treatment: full-stack from day 1; FOS doorstep at 5 DPD
  • Cure target: 35%+ within 1-90 DPD
  • Legal funnel pre-staged at 45 DPD
  • Repo / SARFAESI 13(2) at 75 DPD if secured
